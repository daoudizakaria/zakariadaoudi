Introduction

What is Nuclear Physics ?

Nuclear physics is the field of physics that studies atomic nuclei and their constituents and interactions, in addition to the study of other forms of nuclear matter.
The atom nucleus consists of protons and neutrons. Protons are positively charged and neutrons are neutrally charged. The atomic weight is made up essentially of the atomic nucleus. The task of the neutrons is to hold together the protons. Electrons are negatively charged.
In an atom, the number of electrons is exactly the same as the number of protons in the nucleus.
Radiation is produced when an atom decays. Only atoms with an built-in instability decay spontaneously. These are converted into other elements or isotopes when decaying.
We are interested now by the radioactivity decay. Radioactive decay is a stochastic process where a certain number of nucleus decay to produce at least one daughter nucleide. How ever, the radioactive decay process is stochatistc, this means that is somehow "random". But a big number of radioactive nucleis could make possible the establishment of predictions about the future of that "sample". Let's get back the serious. There are 5 types of radioactive decay :
  1. Alpha decay
  2. Beta Decay
  3. Gamma Decay
  4. Positron Emission
  5. Electron Capture
Furthemore, where are more interested in the 3 first types.

1- Alpha decay α : occurs when the nucleus ejects an alpha particle (helium nucleus).

AZx → A-4Z-2Y + 42He,

where X is the mother nucleide, Y the daughter nucleide and He the Helium nuclei.

2- Beta decays β : occurs in two ways :

a- Beta-minus decay β- : occurs when the nucleides contains more neutrons than protons, the nucleus then emits an electron and an antineutrino in a process that changes a neutron to a proton, this should be summarize by this eqution

10n → 11p + 0-1e + ν,

where n is a neutron, p a proton, 0-1e an electron and ν an antineutrino (this is another story for another article).

b- Beta-plus decay β+ : occurs when the nucleids contains more proton than neutrons. The nucleus emits a positron and a neutrino in a process that changes a proton to a neutron, also known as positron emission, the equation could be written as folow :

11p → 10n + 0+1e + ν ,

where 0+1e is a positron and ν a neutrino.

.

3- Gamma decay γ : when a radioactive nucleus first decays by the emission of an alpha or beta particle. The daughter nucleus that results is usually left in an excited state and it can decay to a lower energy state by emitting a gamma ray photon.

Now give a look at the figure in the right :


This figure represent the Band of Stability or the "Valley", it is the area where stable, non-radioactive isotopes exist based on their ratio of neutrons to protons. To be more explicitly, stable isotopes and nucleids could be defined by the region where, in a nucleide, the number is neutrons N is approximatively equal to the number of protons Z, i.e N ≈ Z, represented by the bold line in the figure. The more the nucleides deviate from this line, the more it is instable. It shows also that the more the nucleide gets heavier, the more likely it is to be unstable.
Add to this, it give us the area where a specific decay type could occurs.
